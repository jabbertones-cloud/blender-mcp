day BirdEye would improve detail scores by
+2-3 points over v16's plain v1 pipeline.

Result: REJECTED. Day BirdEye scored 67-73
(vs v16's 78-80). CLAHE on day scenes:
- Introduces local contrast artifacts
- Shifts exposure away from 0.45 optimal
- Creates unnatural tonal transitions in
  bright overhead daylight shots
- BirdEye edge density may increase but
  other metrics (exposure, noise) degrade

Lesson: CLAHE is strictly a night/low-light
technique. Day scenes should only use the
conservative v1 pipeline.

CRITICAL LEARNINGS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

L1: HYBRID STRATEGY IS OPTIMAL
    Different scenes need different pipelines.
    Day: conservative v1 (autocontrast+gamma+USM)
    Night: aggressive v2 (CLAHE+EDGE_ENHANCE)
    One-size-fits-all loses 5-9 points.

L2: CLAHE IS NIGHT-ONLY
    CLAHE blend=0.15 on day BirdEye: -9 pts.
    CLAHE blend=0.3 on night all cameras: +20 pts.
    The technique amplifies local contrast.
    In daylight: creates artifacts on uniform
    surfaces (road, sky). In darkness: reveals
    hidden detail in shadows.

L3: NIGHT PIPELINE IS 20x MORE EFFECTIVE
    Night v17 gained +20.5 points per scene.
    Day modifications gain 0-3 points max.
    Night is where massive improvements are
    still possible.

    BirdEye detail ceiling (~80 from overhead
    geometry). Further day improvement requires
