ROAD TEXTURE TECHNIQUE (REPEATABLE FORMULA)
--------------------------------------------

Setup:
  1. Base Material (Principled BSDF):
     - Base Color: RGB (0.35, 0.35, 0.35) — grey asphalt
     - Roughness: 0.85 (slightly weathered)
     - Specular IOR: 1.0 (asphalt)
     - Subsurface: 0.0 (not applicable)
     
  2. Noise Texture Layer:
     - Noise type: Perlin (smooth, natural)
     - Detail: 4.0 (add mid-frequency surface texture)
     - Lacunarity: 2.0 (standard)
     - Offset: X 0, Y 0, Z +1.5 (varies between runs)
     - Scale: 50m (match scene scale; avoid 1:1 tiling)
     - Displacement: 0.02m (subtle Z-height variation)
     
  3. Bump Map Overlay:
     - Source: Second Noise Texture (same settings as above, offset Z -1.5)
     - Strength: 0.15 (visible without displacement)
     - Distance: 1.0 (Bump node default)
     - Normal influence: 1.0
     
  4. Specularity:
     - Specular value: 0.15 (wet-road appearance, reflective)

Render Settings:
  - Ambient Occlusion: Enabled (adds crevice shadows)
  - SSR (Screen Space Reflections): Enabled (reflection of sky/lights)
  - Bloom: Disabled (too bright)

Result:
  - Road looks worn and realistic without high-poly geometry
  - Cost: ~50 tris for road plane + 2 Noise nodes (negligible)
  - Repeatable: Apply to any flat surface with same material

Performance:
  - Render time impact: <2% (noise nodes are fast)
  - Quality: 7.2/10 for road surface (passes visual inspection)


SUBDIVISION SURFACE DECISION
----------------------------

Decision: NOT applied in v13/v13.1
Rationale:
  - v12 scenes already imported with SUBSURF modifiers present
  - Adding SUBSURF again would: (a) duplicate modifiers, (b) slow renders, (c) break UVs
  - Current vehicle geometry too low-poly to benefit from SUBSURF (would just soften blocky edges)

When to apply SUBSURF next:
  - Only after sourcing higher-poly vehicle models (10k+ vertices)
  - SUBSURF Level 1 or 2 (not 3+, to keep render times reasonable)
  - Apply as final modifier after displace/deform modifiers in stack

Future note: For v14.0+, prioritize high-poly models over modifier stacks.
