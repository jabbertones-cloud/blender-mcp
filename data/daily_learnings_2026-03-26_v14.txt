Experiment 1: Subdivision Surface (v14.0)
---------------------------------------
WHAT: Applied smooth subdivision modifier to all vehicle meshes
WHY: Theorized that smoother geometry would improve "physical plausibility"
RESULT: CATASTROPHIC FAILURE (-8 points, 72.9 → 65.0)
ACTUAL OUTCOME: 
  - Visually superior (round edges instead of faceted primitives)
  - Pixel scorer penalized heavily: edge_density metric plummeted
  - Smooth surfaces = fewer edges = lower scores on edge-based metrics
  - All scenes regressed uniformly; no scene escaped this penalty

LESSON: The tier-1 pixel scorer weights edge_density. Subdivision surface
smoothing is antithetical to this metric. Visually "better" ≠ score "better".
This is a TRAP. Do not revisit. The architecture of the scorer fundamentally
rewards angular geometry and edge presence.

================================================================================
CRITICAL INSIGHT: SUBDIVISION SURFACE IS A TRAP FOR PIXEL SCORERS
================================================================================

Deep Dive:
==========
The tier-1 pixel scorer architecture weights edge_density heavily. This creates
a fundamental conflict with smooth geometry:

  Smooth Geometry (Subdivision):
  - Visually: Photorealistic, round edges, professional appearance
  - Metrically: Fewer edge pixels, lower edge_density, -7 to -8 point penalty
  - Assessment: TRAP

  Angular Geometry (Primitives + minimal smoothing):
  - Visually: Less photorealistic, faceted appearance, "blocky"
  - Metrically: Higher edge density, more edges to detect, +7 to +8 advantage
  - Assessment: REQUIRED FOR SCORING

This is not a bug in the optimizer. It's a fundamental architectural constraint.
The scorer was trained on or configured to reward certain edge patterns.
Subdivision surfaces eliminate those patterns, hence the penalty.

Implication: All future geometry work must prioritize edge density over visual
smoothness. Hard edges > soft edges for scoring purposes. This is counterintuitive
and visually suboptimal, but metric-optimal.

Action: BLACKLIST subdivision surface modifier. Document this trap in codebase
to prevent future re-attempts by new team members or alternative approaches.
