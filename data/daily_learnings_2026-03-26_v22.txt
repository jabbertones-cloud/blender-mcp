CRITICAL LEARNINGS (5 Key Insights)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

L1: EEVEE CANNOT RENDER IN --BACKGROUND MODE
    Blender 5.1 EEVEE requires GPU context for texture operations.
    --background mode fails silently: "ERROR Failed to create GPU texture"
    Result: black or flat-white frames.
    WORKAROUND: Use MCP bridge (TCP socket to Blender with GUI running)
    or switch to Cycles engine for CLI rendering.

L2: SELECTIVE DENOISING > BLANKET DENOISING
    Blanket 3-layer denoising: -4.93 average regression
    Selective merge (denoised only where better): +1.14 improvement
    Rule: ONLY apply denoising to cameras with measured noise > 0.3
    AND verify score improvement before committing.

L3: NOISE THRESHOLD FOR DENOISING BENEFIT
    Cameras with noise > 5.0: denoising helps significantly (+2 to +4)
    Cameras with noise 0.01-0.5: denoising causes small regression (-1 to -5)
    Cameras with noise < 0.01: denoising causes large regression (-6 to -12)
    The scorer penalizes detail loss more than noise presence.

L4: BEST-OF MERGE IS THE OPTIMAL POST-PROCESSING STRATEGY
    For each camera: render → measure → denoise → score → keep best
    This per-camera decision tree ensures zero regressions.
    Should be formalized into the pipeline for future iterations.

L5: DIMINISHING RETURNS AT 96+ SCORE LEVEL
    The gap between 95 and 100 requires render-time improvements,
    not post-processing. Remaining bottlenecks:
    - scene1_BirdEye (92): needs Blender lighting/camera changes
    - scene3_BirdEye (90): needs scene geometry improvements
    - scene1_DriverPOV (91): needs interior detail modeling
