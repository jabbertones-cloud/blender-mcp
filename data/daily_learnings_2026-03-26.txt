CRITICAL LEARNINGS (ADD TO KNOWLEDGE BASE)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

L1: PIXEL SCORER REWARDS SIMPLICITY, NOT FIDELITY
    The tier-1 scorer fundamentally rewards:
    - Clean, high-contrast compositions
    - Large uniform areas with sharp edges
    - Minimal visual clutter
    It PENALIZES:
    - Complex geometry (creates visual noise)
    - Dense object placement (reduces contrast)
    - Detailed surfaces (lower edge_density clarity)
    IMPLICATION: 3D scene improvements have hit ceiling.

L2: ADDING OBJECTS ALWAYS HURTS DAY SCENE SCORES
    Every tested addition (vehicles, road markings, parked cars,
    sidewalks, curbs, crosswalks) caused regression. The v13.1
    minimalist base is at or near the scorer's optimum for day.

L3: NIGHT SCENES ARE THE ONLY SCENE-LEVEL IMPROVEMENT VECTOR
    High-poly vehicles + night lighting = reflections + edge
    density that the scorer rewards. SecurityCam hit 82 — the
    highest single camera score in project history.

L4: FREE HIGH-POLY VEHICLE SOURCING IS SOLVED
    bounverif/openx-assets (GitHub, MPL-2.0/CC-BY-4.0):
    12+ real vehicles (BMW, Audi, Mercedes, Volvo, GMC, etc.)
    3-8MB .blend files, 36-90 objects per vehicle.
    OpenGameArt: 14+ vehicles in .blend/.fbx format.
    All downloaded to assets/free_models/.

L5: SCORER CEILING IS ~75-76 WITH CURRENT APPROACH
    Day: ~75 (BirdEye 64-68, DriverPOV 78-81, Wide 72-78)
    Night: ~76.5 (SecurityCam peaks at 82)
    Overall: ~75-76
    Gap to 85: ~10 points — CANNOT be closed by scene mods.
